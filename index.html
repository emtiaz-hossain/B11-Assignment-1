The Vision Of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

Dr. Muhammad Yunus is a Bangladeshi economist and social entrepreneur best known for pioneering microfinance and founding the Grameen Bank. His work has transformed the fight against poverty and earned him the Nobel Peace Prize in 2006.Born in 1940 in Chittagong, Bangladesh, Yunus studied economics at Dhaka University before earning his Ph.D. at Vanderbilt University in the United States. Returning home, he witnessed the devastating effects of poverty and famine in the 1970s. Convinced that traditional banking excluded the poor, he began lending small amounts of money to basket weavers and villagers, enabling them to buy materials and start businesses. This experiment grew into the Grameen Bank, formally established in 1983.
